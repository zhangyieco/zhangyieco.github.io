About

Welcome. I am a Ph.D. candidate in Social Security at Shanghai University of Finance and Economics (SUFE,上海财经大学).

My research interests are health economics and industrial organization.

My dissertation discusses the impact of China's long-term care insurance on the entry decisions of service providers.

I employ empirical methods, including difference-in-differences, instrumental variables, and structural models, to study policy evaluation and market dynamics in healthcare and long-term care markets.

My work has been published in peer-reviewed journals such as Journal of Asian Economics, 社会科学, 财经研究, and 经济学（季刊）.

You can find my full list of publications and working papers in the Publications & Working Papers section.

Research Interests

  • Health Economics: Long-term care insurance, healthcare reform, pharmaceutical markets
  • Industrial Organization: Market entry, competition, regulation
  • Applied Microeconomics: Policy evaluation, program impact, rural development
