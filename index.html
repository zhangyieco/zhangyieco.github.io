Biography

Welcome! I am a Ph.D. candidate in Social Security at Shanghai University of Finance and Economics (SUFE, 上海财经大学). My research focuses on health economics and industrial organization, with particular interest in the impact of public policies on healthcare markets.

My dissertation examines the effects of China's long-term care insurance pilot program on the entry decisions of service providers. This work combines econometric analysis with industrial organization theory to understand how insurance expansion affects market structure and consumer welfare.

I am also involved in projects studying peer effects in healthcare utilization and the supply-side responses to health insurance expansions.

Selected Publications

The impact of parental migration on non-cognitive abilities of left behind children in northwestern China

Han Liu, Fang Chang, Hannah Corn, Yi Zhang, Yaojiang Shi

Journal of Asian Economics, 2021

Despite the vast literature surrounding various aspects of left behind children (LBC)'s development in China, very few studies have examined the development of, and impact on their non-cognitive abilities as a result of parental migration. Using survey data consisting of 5002 eighth graders from 160 middle schools in northwestern China, this paper investigates how parental migration affects children's non-cognitive abilities.

长期护理保险与老年照护机构供给：基于S市试点的证据

余央央，张毅，封进

社会科学, 2021

引导照护供给资源合理配置是长护险制度可持续发展的关键问题之一。文章利用长护险某试点城市1152家照护机构数据，考察待遇给付模式对不同类型照护机构供给的影响。采用双重差分法的实证结果显示，优先针对居家照护提供待遇给付会引导此类机构供给率先增加。
